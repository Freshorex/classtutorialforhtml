Modeling and 2D work!


I've always loved to make illustrations and to design characters, locations, whatever my mind could conjure up.

My love for writing has taken into a path of creativity down a rabbit hole of possibilities: drawings, concept art, AND 3D artwork/models and how they're made.

Due to this, I've become quite a 3D nerd, watching content related to stuff like this, but why should I speak, when the artworks can work for themselves?
I may not be an expert on 3D models yet, but I'm learning, and I'm willing to learn a lot more.


Programs I've used for my 3D work

Blender

It's a pretty commonly used and open-source platform, and it can ALSO be used for: video compositing and editing; sculpting; 2D animation; and more... I'ts mostly used in 3D animation and modelling but many artists (such as myself) use both 2D and 3D mediums together to explore more possibilities.


Programs I've used for my 2D work

Adobe Illustrator/Inkscape

For my vector art! I've made quite a few illustrations with Illustrator but thanks to Inkscape I got used to work with these type of projects, long live SVGs! Lol.

Krita

While I don't use Krita as often anymore, it's still a great program! Used to animate on it often, and some of the artworks I made there were beautiful!

Magma (previously Aggie)

Probably the program (site) I use the most! Drawing on it feels so smooth and it's so comfortable to use! I LOVE using it, and I will keep using it for years to come as long as it lives and breathes... metaphorically speaking, of course.
